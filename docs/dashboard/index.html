Variable Indication
Perfluorodecanoic acid (PFDA) Environmental exposure is associated with chronic inflammation; potential links to cancer, developmental disorders, and other diseases are currently being explored. [1]
White blood cell (WBC) count High WBC count is indicative of inflammation in inflammatory or necrotic conditions [2]
C-reactive protein (CRP) High CRP is associated with chronic inflammation. [3]
Albumin Low albumin levels are associated with inflammation and Rheumatoid arthritis. [3]
  1. Kaiser JP, Lizarraga L, Arzuaga X, et al. IRIS Toxicological Review of Perfluorodecanoic Acid (PFDA) and Related Salts CASRN 335-76-2. Washington (DC): U.S. Environmental Protection Agency; 2024 Jul. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY. Available from: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK610882/#
  2. Berliner S, Caspi D, Neuman Y, et al. Aggregation of white cells and C-reactive protein: relation between these two indices in acute phase reaction. Journal of Clinical Pathology. 1987;40:103-106.
  3. Sheinenzon A, Shehadeh M, Michelis R, et al. Serum albumin levels and inflammation. International Journal of Biological Macromolecules. 2021;184:857-862.
